# THE OPEN SOURCE JOURNAL DOCTRINE

**Published:** 19 July 2026
**Promulgated by:** Ronav Isrgrajan Narayandas (also known as Ratio Juris)

---

## PREAMBLE

The Open Source Journal, acting in good faith to protect humanity and the immutable wisdom of knowledge, hereby establishes this Doctrine.

The Open Source Journal values knowledge and the profound wisdom that a human mind can craft. It recognises the human being: not gender, not colour, not language. It recognises pure knowledge: not the degree, not the institutional reputation, not the academic scorecard. It stands as a universal declaration that truth requires no pedigree, and that the fundamental right to share, learn, and elevate the human condition must remain forever free, uncompromising, and unobstructed.

## INTRODUCTION

Knowledge is the fundamental architecture of human progress and the immutable heritage of humanity. By its nature, true intellectual advancement outpaces the rigid institutions constructed to contain, certify, or commodify it.

Historically and systemically, the dissemination of intellectual breakthroughs has been subjected to artificial scarcity. A paradigm of institutional gatekeeping has become entrenched, in which access to knowledge, and the validation of it, is dictated not by empirical merit but by financial capital, credentialed pedigree, and exclusive membership.<sup id="fnref1"><a class="fnref" href="#fn1">[1]</a></sup> This systemic enclosure extracts commercial profit from foundational intellectual labour, erecting structural barriers between the independent creator and the global public.

This structural flaw extends across both academic publishing and technological infrastructure alike. The validation of academic research, software logic, and empirical data is frequently subordinated to purchased certificates, subscription paywalls, and institutional trust stores.<sup id="fnref2"><a class="fnref" href="#fn2">[2]</a></sup> These mechanisms evaluate the financial capacity and institutional affiliation of the author rather than the objective execution and validity of the work itself. Consequently, the independent scholar and the unfunded developer are systematically disenfranchised, obstructed by arbitrary parameters that prioritise institutional gatekeeping over the advancement of human understanding.

To cure this structural mischief, to dismantle the commercial and academic enclosure of information, and to replace credential-based filtration with proof-of-execution, this framework is hereby enacted. The Open Source Journal establishes that trust must be earned through open, empirical scrutiny rather than purchased through institutional mandate, so that the transmission of knowledge remains a universal right rather than a licensed privilege.

## CHAPTER I: PRELIMINARY

### 1. Short Title, Extent, and Commencement

(1) This framework shall be known and cited as **The Open Source Journal Doctrine** (referred to herein as "this Doctrine").

(2) This Doctrine applies to every Manuscript published under it and to every person, organisation, or Node that adopts it, without regard to geography, nationality, or region. It does not purport to override the sovereign legal authority of any Nation-State. Where a co-operating Nation-State's law does not compel the reintroduction of Gatekeeping as defined herein, that law is respected in full; where it does, this Doctrine continues to bind those who have voluntarily adopted it, to the fullest extent that adoption can lawfully operate.

(3) This Doctrine comes into force upon its publication and continues perpetually, subject only to Chapter VII (Severability).

### 2. Interpretation and Definitions

In this framework, unless the context otherwise requires:

- **(a) "The Open Source Journal"** (also "The OSJ" or "The OS Journal") means the regulatory entity and philosophical foundation governing the autonomous transmission of knowledge under this Doctrine.
- **(b) "The Soul"** means the immutable philosophical and jurisdictional core of this Doctrine, identified precisely in Section 4(4). It is the unchangeable substance that governs the open transmission of knowledge and the protection of the author's verbatim intent, as authored and originally promulgated by Ratio Juris.
- **(c) "The Open Source Journal License"** (also "The OSJ License" or "The OSJL") means the distinct, subordinate legal instrument, constrained by the Soul, which governs the specific usage, access, and distribution of individual works published under this Doctrine.
- **(d) "The OSJ Rules"** means the internal regulatory, governance, and administrative rules enacted by consensus of the OSJ Members, constrained by the Soul.
- **(e) "Manuscript"** means any textual, mathematical, computational, or empirical work submitted for public education, peer replication, or validation under this Doctrine.
- **(f) "Gatekeeping"** means the restriction of publication, access, execution, or reading of a Manuscript by any academic, non-academic, or technological institution on the basis of eligibility prerequisites, monetary payment, credentials, or membership status. Gatekeeping does not include voluntary, non-exclusionary quality indicators, moderation of spam or unlawful content, or minimal technical measures necessary for network security, provided none of these is used as a pretext for credential-based or financial exclusion.
- **(g) "Absolute Justice" and "Judicial Justice"** carry the meanings given to them in the separately published *[Doctrine of Judicial Justice and Absolute Justice](./JJAJ.md)*. Where that instrument is inaccessible at the point of reference, or does not yet define a term this Doctrine relies upon, "Absolute Justice" and "Judicial Justice" shall be read in their ordinary sense, in furtherance of the Preamble and Introduction of this Doctrine, and in favour of the independent creator over the institutional or commercial gatekeeper.
- **(h) "Node"** means any person, machine, server, or organisation that stores, mirrors, or transmits a Manuscript under this Doctrine.

### 3. Rules of Interpretation

Any ambiguity in this Doctrine, The OSJ Rules, or The OSJ License shall be resolved in the following descending order:

(1) The literal words of the provision, read in their ordinary meaning.

(2) Where a literal reading would defeat the Preamble or Introduction, or would permit Gatekeeping in substance while avoiding it in form, the reading that prevents that outcome.

(3) Where more than one reading survives (1) and (2), the reading that most favours open access, the independent creator, and the abolition of institutional gatekeeping.

## CHAPTER II: THE SOUL, THE BODY, AND IMMUTABILITY

### 4. The Soul and the Body

(1) The Open Source Journal operates, conceptually and structurally, as a soul with a body.

(2) **The Soul** is the supreme, unalterable philosophical core establishing the principles of free knowledge and the abolition of Gatekeeping.

(3) **The Body** is The OSJ Rules and The OSJ License: the operational and legal apparatus through which the Soul interacts with the world, manages Manuscripts, and governs the organisation.

(4) **Scope of the Soul.** For the removal of doubt, the Soul consists precisely of: Chapter I; this Chapter II; Chapter IV; Chapter V; and Section 12 of Chapter VI. Every other provision of this Doctrine, being Chapter III, Section 13 of Chapter VI, and Chapter VII, together with The OSJ Rules and The OSJ License, constitutes the Body, and may be amended as Section 5 provides, strictly within the limits the Soul imposes. Where any provision of the Body conflicts with the Soul, the Soul prevails and the conflicting provision is void to the extent of the conflict.

### 5. Absolute Immutability of the Soul

(1) No provision exists, and none shall ever be created, to change, amend, overwrite, or dilute the Soul.

(2) The Body may be amended or adapted over time by The OSJ to meet evolving technological and societal demands. The Soul remains permanently immutable. No rule, licence update, or committee decision may supersede or alter it.

### 6. The Regulator of the OS Journal

(1) The OSJ is the sole regulatory authority of the open knowledge framework established by this Doctrine.

(2) The OSJ bears responsibility for maintaining the accuracy, integrity, and enforcement of this Doctrine.

(3) The OSJ has the exclusive right to amend The OSJ License and The OSJ Rules over time, strictly within the limitations the Soul sets.

## CHAPTER III: SOVEREIGNTY, COMMITTEES, AND GOVERNANCE

### 7. Respect for National Sovereignty

The OSJ respects the sovereignty and integrity of each Nation-State that believes in knowledge sharing, knowledge acquisition, and the progressive use of science and technology as it evolves with time, on the terms set out in Section 1(2).

### 8. Establishment of the Committees

To bring domestic laws into international alignment under The OSJ License, the following bodies are established:

(1) **The OSJ Sovereign National Committee.** Established at host-nation level, responsible for drafting, proposing, and ensuring the enforceability of copyright and intellectual property matters within its country.

(2) **The OSJ Sovereign Committee.** The supreme international governing body. Each OSJ Sovereign National Committee presents its proposed rules to the OSJ Sovereign Committee.

(3) **Final Authority on Rules.** The OSJ Sovereign Committee holds final authority on all proposed OSJ Rules. No rule is final or internationally binding until formally notified by The OSJ, and no rule may contradict the rights the Soul mandates.

### 9. Appointment and Dilution of the Chair

(1) **Composition.** The OSJ Sovereign Committee is headed by the OSJ Chairman, together with the President (Co-Chairman), the Vice President, and the Executive Members of the States.

(2) **Initial Appointments.** The initial appointments to the committees are made exclusively by the OSJ Chairman, Ratio Juris.

(3) **Dilution of the Chairman's Seat.** Upon the demission, vacation of office, dismissal, or demise of Ratio Juris, the seat of the OSJ Chairman is permanently diluted. The seat remains perpetually empty, and no individual shall ever again be appointed to it. This dilution is deliberate and belongs to the Soul, not the Body: it exists so that the OSJ itself never becomes a permanent institutional gatekeeper commanding singular authority in perpetuity, which would betray the purpose for which this Doctrine exists.

(4) **Succession of Authority.** Once the Chairman's seat is vacated, the President, acting as Co-Chairman, exercises the powers previously vested in the Chairman. The President does not hold personal final say; the President exercises those powers only as directed by, and strictly subject to, the consensus of the OSJ Members. Future appointments follow the same consensus.

(5) **State-Level Appointments.** At host-nation level, the National Committee may be proposed by the host nation, appointed by The OSJ, or both. The final decision on state-level appointments rests with the Chairman or, after dilution, with the President acting under Section 9(4).

### 10. Internal Law

(1) The OSJ holds the right to enact internal laws (The OSJ Rules) for its own governance, based on the consensus of its Members.

(2) The provisions of this Chapter III that describe the governance structure, internal lawmaking, and the perpetual dilution of the Chairman's seat under Section 9(3) may not be amended, and cannot be overwritten by any rule of The OSJ License, once the founding Chairman vacates office. This restriction protects Section 9(3) specifically; it does not, by itself, elevate the remainder of this Chapter to the Soul, whose scope is fixed exclusively by Section 4(4).

## CHAPTER IV: THE IMMUTABLE RIGHTS OF THE AUTHOR

### 11. The Sanctity of Verbatim Intent

(1) The OSJ recognises that the true architect of knowledge is the author. To prevent the intellectual supersession, theft, or adulteration of an independent creator's work, the Soul establishes the author's verbatim intent as inviolable.

(2) This principle forms the jurisprudential bedrock for The OSJ License, which shall forbid the unauthorised alteration, modification, or uncredited reproduction of any submitted Manuscript, and shall require that attribution to the original author accompany every copy, translation, and derivative.

(3) No provision of The OSJ Rules or The OSJ License may weaken the protection this Section affords. Any provision that purports to do so is void to that extent, under Section 4(4).

## CHAPTER V: THE ABOLITION OF GATEKEEPING

### 12. Prohibition of Institutional Barriers

(1) The OSJ shall facilitate, maintain, and ensure the eradication of Gatekeeping by any academic or non-academic institution.

(2) No institution, platform, publisher, or trust authority may impose an eligibility prerequisite, demand financial payment, or require exclusive membership as a condition of publishing, accessing, or reading any knowledge protected under this framework. This prohibition does not extend to the exceptions set out in the definition of Gatekeeping at Section 2(f).

(3) Knowledge regulated by The OS Journal is evaluated strictly on empirical merit, logical coherence, and proof-of-execution. Institutional pedigree and purchased credentials are irrelevant to that evaluation and carry no weight under this Doctrine.

## CHAPTER VI: JURISPRUDENCE AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION

### 13. Jurisprudence

The jurisprudence of this Doctrine is drawn from the concepts of Judicial Justice and Absolute Justice, as defined in Section 2(g). It recognises that obstructing fundamental knowledge for commercial or institutional advantage is unjust, and it establishes a mandate to secure the freedom of information accordingly.

### 14. Dispute Resolution

Any dispute concerning the interpretation, governance, or enforcement of this Doctrine, The OSJ Rules, or The OSJ License, whether internal or external, is resolved by the consensus of the OSJ Members, applying the Rules of Interpretation in Section 3. The decision reached by that consensus is final, save that no such decision may alter, dilute, or contradict the Soul.

## CHAPTER VII: SEVERABILITY

### 15. Severability Clause

If any provision of The OSJ Rules or The OSJ License is found unenforceable, technologically obstructed, or in irreconcilable conflict with the supreme law of a co-operating Nation-State, that provision alone is severed. The remainder of this Doctrine, and the Soul in full, remains in force and undiminished in authority. No severance under this Section may be used to achieve, in effect, an amendment that Section 5 or Section 10(2) would otherwise forbid.

*End of The Open Source Journal Doctrine.*
