# The Open Source Journal (OSJ) Doctrine

**Author:** Ratio Juris (Ronav Isrgrajan Narayandas)
**License:** [OSJ License v1.0](./LICENSE.md) ([plain text](./LICENSE.txt)) — a custom license, not MIT/CC, written to match the doctrine's own anti-gatekeeping logic.

The OSJ Doctrine is a manifesto written in the form of a legal statute. It argues that two systems — academic publishing and software trust infrastructure — share the same underlying defect: both judge a work by who is allowed to vouch for it (a university, a publisher, a paid Certificate Authority) rather than by what the work actually does.

Instead, the OSJ proposes **proof-of-execution**: a doctrine, paper, or program earns trust by surviving open, adversarial, public scrutiny — not by arriving with a credential, a subscription fee, or a purchased certificate.

It is written in the register of a statute (Preamble, Definitions, Rules of Interpretation, Recitals, Overrides) deliberately, as a rhetorical device — it is a piece of applied philosophy and rhetoric, not an enforceable legal code, and it does not claim otherwise.

## Core argument, in short

- **Academic publishing:** A small number of commercial publishers control both submission and access, extracting high margins from labor researchers already performed for free — while roughly 72% of scholarly output remains behind a paywall.
- **Software trust:** Independent, actively-maintained code is flagged "untrusted" by default unless its author pays for a Certificate Authority–issued signing certificate — a cost that has nothing to do with whether the code is safe or well-written.
- **The pattern is the same:** both systems substitute a *credential check* for an *evaluation of the work itself*. The OSJ Doctrine names this pattern and proposes a decentralized, citation-backed alternative.

## Why the OSJ License instead of MIT/CC

A doctrine written against gatekeeping shouldn't ship under a license built to protect institutions. The OSJ License guarantees the work stays free to read forever, cannot be re-paywalled by anyone downstream, and requires attribution — while still allowing forks, translation, and critical extension. See [`LICENSE.md`](./LICENSE.md) / [`LICENSE.txt`](./LICENSE.txt) for the full text.
